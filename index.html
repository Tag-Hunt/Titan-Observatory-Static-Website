Our Mission Plan

We are building Titan Observatory as a nonprofit, community telescope dedicated to open science. Every dollar we raise moves this instrument from retirement back to discovery and puts powerful tools into the hands of students, researchers, and curious explorers everywhere.

1. Secure the Telescope

Finalize the purchase of the retired NASA radio dish, complete due diligence, and document its current condition with the help of engineers and historians.

2. Relocate and Restore

Transport the telescope to a dark-sky site, rebuild critical systems, and outfit the facility with modern receivers and control hardware.

3. Build the Community Program

Create an online reservation system, publish training resources, and schedule open office hours so anyone can learn how to use Titan efficiently.

4. Deliver Open Data

Automate data pipelines so observations are processed, archived, and delivered to observers with reproducible documentation and support.

What Happens Next

We are moving fast, and you can track the next milestones below. Progress updates land in Discord first, followed by our newsletter.

  • Finalize acquisition logistics: lock in funding commitments, inspection dates, and agreements with the current owner.
  • Select relocation site: confirm zoning, power, and fiber access at candidate locations and begin the permitting process.
  • Design restoration plan: scope mechanical and electrical upgrades, budget the work, and recruit specialist volunteers.
  • Launch early-access program: invite educators, researchers, and citizen scientists to help define the first observing campaigns.
