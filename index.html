Our Mission Plan

We are building Titan Astronomical Observatory as a nonprofit, community telescope dedicated to open science. Every dollar we raise moves this instrument from retirement back to discovery and puts powerful tools into the hands of students, researchers, and curious explorers everywhere.

1. Preparation & Greenlight

Initiate fundraising, run RF interference sweeps on potential relocation sites, and obtain permits so the new site is legally ready to host Titan.

2. Disassembly & Relocation

Disassembly of dish and mount, transportation to new site, and reassembly and installation atop professionally engineered footing.

3. Outfit for Open Access

Update depricated hardware and employ custom software to allow for online reservation and automatic data collection.

4. Operations & Maintenance

Open Titan for public use. Ensure constant operations and regular maintenance.

What Happens Next

We are moving fast, and you can track the next milestones below. Progress updates land in Discord first, followed by our newsletter.

  • Finalize acquisition logistics: lock in funding commitments, inspection dates, and agreements with the current owner.
  • Select relocation site: confirm zoning, power, and fiber access at candidate locations and begin the permitting process.
  • Design restoration plan: scope mechanical and electrical upgrades, budget the work, and recruit specialist volunteers.
  • Launch early-access program: invite educators, researchers, and citizen scientists to help define the first observing campaigns.

Volunteer with Us

Hands-on restoration work and remote roles are both open. We're especially looking for people who can keep our RF systems quiet and our software running smoothly.

  • RF and interference specialists to tune receivers, run spectrum surveys, and mitigate noise.
  • Programmers to maintain and extend the control and data software already written for Titan.
  • Community and education leads to help new observers get on the air.
